Because in our laboratory we can not centrifuge in a high temperature, we ignore the last several steps: ‘Molten liquid was separated from the crystals in a centrifuge, with silica wool serving as a filter. Remaining flux that attached to the surface of crystals was removed by keeping the crystal at 500 ◦C for 3 days in a sealed silica tube while leaving the cold end of the tube at room temperature.’

Then we set up the calcination program, waiting for 5~6 days.
